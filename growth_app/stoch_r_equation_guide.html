Equation Guide
  • \(N\) is the population size at a given time-step (\(t\))
  • \(r_d\) is the mean discrete growth rate, drawn from a normal distribution each time-step
    • \(r = 0\): no change
    • \(r > 0\): positive growth
    • \(r < 0\): population decline
  • \(K\) is the carrying capacity
  • \(r\) SD is the standard deviation of the normal distribution from which \(r_d\) is drawn

In this model, stochasticity is introduced by drawing the value of \(r\) from a Gaussian distribution at each time-step.
