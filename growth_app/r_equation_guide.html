Equation Guide
  • \(N\) is the population size at a given time-step (\(t\))
  • \(r_d\) is the discrete growth rate
    • \(r = 0\): no change
    • \(r > 0\): positive growth
    • \(r < 0\): population decline
  • \(K\) is the carrying capacity
Limit Cycles and Chaos
  • \(r_d < 2.000\): population reaches \(K\) with dampening oscillations
  • \(2.000 < r_d < 2.449\): two-point stable limit cycle
  • \(2.449 < r_d < 2.570\): limit cycles increase geometrically in period (2, 4, 8, 16…)
  • \(r_d > 2.570\): aperiodic (irregular) patterns known as chaos
